Executive Summary


House of Grønli ©™ is a Private Membership Association (PMA) operating a sovereign administrative infrastructure for the private management of trademarked assets, member identity, and internal financial settlement. This white paper describes the complete technical and legal architecture of the PMA Utility — a purpose-built digital terminal enabling members to manage their private interests through a cryptographically secured, standards-compliant private clearinghouse.

The system is built on three foundational pillars:
A pre-existing registered Trademark (House of Grønli ©™) serving as the immutable Primary Identity Anchor for all private network operations
Non-transferable Soulbound NFT (SBT) credentials gating access to the member terminal and authorising Private Power of Attorney (PPOA) for administrative record-keeping
An ISO 20022-structured private settlement bus enabling member-to-member ledger reconciliation through a centralised Trust Account Number (TAN) system

The Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) “Indre Stambane 5it” operates as a ring-fenced sub-entity within this infrastructure, providing a dedicated private clearinghouse for infrastructure project capital management, asset registration, and distribution reconciliation.

1. Association Overview


1.1 House of Grønli ©™ — Identity & Mission
House of Grønli ©™ is a Private Membership Association established to provide its members with a sovereign, private administrative infrastructure for managing trademarked assets, private trust interests, and internal settlement obligations. The Association operates exclusively within its own private domain — not as a public financial institution, and not subject to public regulatory frameworks applicable to commercial banks or licensed payment service providers.
The Association’s registered trademark predates all secondary entity registrations by a minimum of nine (9) months, establishing temporal priority and seniority of lien interest across all commercial interactions referencing the House of Grønli identity.
1.2 Private Membership Model
Membership in the Association is governed by a private membership agreement. Access to the digital terminal, asset registry, and settlement system requires:
Execution of a Private Membership Agreement
Submission and notarisation of a Private Power of Attorney (PPOA)
Issuance of a Soulbound NFT membership credential by the Association’s issuer multisig
Activation of a Trust Account Number (TAN) in the private ledger

The Association does not hold member private keys. All cryptographic operations are performed exclusively by the member’s own wallet.
1.3 Trademark as Primary Identity Anchor
The trademark House of Grønli ©™ functions as the root identity credential for the entire private network. It is registered on-chain at genesis via the TrustIdentityAnchor contract and referenced by all downstream contracts (SBT, Asset Registry, Ledger Engine). This creates a single, immutable chain of identity from trademark registration to every ledger entry, asset token, and settlement message produced by the system.
Within the private Hyperledger Besu subnet this functions analogously to a self-sovereign Decentralised Identifier (DID), using the method prefix did:pma: and the trademark registration number as the method-specific identifier.

2. Legal & Structural Framework


2.1 Private Trust Structure
The House of Grønli Trust is the controlling legal entity. The Trust holds the trademark as a primary asset, acts as Senior Secured Party in all internal commercial interactions, and is represented by the Trustee in all administrative and operational capacities.
The Trust’s administrative structure is reflected in the C/O (In Care Of) metadata embedded in all ISO 20022-structured internal messages, ensuring that every settlement instruction, account statement, and ledger entry accurately reflects the senior lien interest.
2.2 Private Power of Attorney (PPOA)
The PPOA is a notarised legal instrument granting the Association’s Administrative Trustee limited authority to act on behalf of a member for the sole purpose of:
Signing internal ledger entries
Registering trademark asset tokens in the private registry
Initiating and closing TAN reconciliation cycles

The PPOA is encrypted with the member’s public key, pinned to the private IPFS cluster, and its document hash is stored on-chain in the member’s SBT record. Every write operation to the private network requires the member to produce a valid EIP-712 typed-data signature referencing their active PPOA hash, proving live key control.
PPOA Scope Limitation
The Private Power of Attorney confers NO authority outside the Association’s private administrative domain.
It is NOT a general power of attorney.
It grants no rights to operate in public financial markets on behalf of the member.

2.3 Special Purpose Vehicle — Indre Stambane 5it
The Indre Stambane 5it SPV is a ring-fenced sub-entity within the PMA’s private clearinghouse. It maintains its own TAN sub-account (child of the Trust’s master TAN), its own asset token pool, and an independent reconciliation cycle — while inheriting the Trust’s trademark identity anchor and lien seniority.

3.2.1 TrustIdentityAnchor Contract
The TrustIdentityAnchor contract is deployed first, at genesis. It registers the trademark registration number, jurisdiction, and date as immutable on-chain state. It exposes a priorityGap() function that proves the nine-month temporal seniority of the trademark over any comparison date (e.g., SPV formation date). All downstream contracts call authorizedConsumers to verify they are permitted to reference this anchor.
3.2.2 Soulbound NFT (ERC-5114)
The PMA_SBT contract implements the ERC-5114 Soulbound Token standard. Each token is permanently bound to the minting wallet address. All transfer, approval, and setApprovalForAll functions are overridden to revert unconditionally. Token metadata includes: Member ID, tier (Observer / Member / Administrator), issuance timestamp, PPOA document hash, and revocation status.

3.2.3 Asset Registry
Each trademarked asset is represented as an ERC-721 token. Token metadata references the trademark registration number, class, registration date, owner’s TAN, and the IPFS CID of the encrypted trademark certificate PDF. Assets may only be minted by wallets holding an Active Administrator-tier SBT or presenting a valid PPOA session signature.
3.2.4 Ledger Engine & TAN System
The LedgerEngine implements double-entry bookkeeping on-chain. Every entry requires a debit account, credit account, amount (18-decimal fixed point), memo hash, and PPOA session proof. Entries are immutable once confirmed; corrections are posted as reversing entries.
The Trust Account Number (TAN) format is: PMA-[CHAIN_PREFIX]-[MEMBER_SEQ_HEX]-[CHECKSUM]. It is derived deterministically at SBT issuance: keccak256(memberAddress + issuedAt + chainId). The TAN supports a parent-child hierarchy for SPV sub-accounts.

4. ISO 20022 Private Settlement Layer


4.1 Architecture
The ISO 20022 mapping operates exclusively as internal middleware within the private clearinghouse. It transforms on-chain ledger state into ISO 20022-structured XML envelopes for member-to-member settlement instructions. These messages never reach a public clearinghouse unless a member explicitly initiates an outbound gateway transaction, at which point all private identifiers are substituted with real public-network credentials.
Private vs. Public Identifier Boundary
Inside the PMA network:  scheme = PMA_TRADEMARK_ANCHOR / PMA_TAN
Outside the PMA network: scheme = GLEIF LEI / IBAN (real public credentials)
The Gateway Adapter enforces this substitution on every outbound message.

5. Member Terminal


5.1 Authentication Flow
The terminal enforces a five-step authentication sequence before any session is established. No persistent session tokens are stored; all session state is memory-only and expires per the PPOA expiry field.
Wallet connection via MetaMask or WalletConnect
SBT presence check: balanceOf(connectedAddress) must return 1
SBT status check: memberRecords[address].revoked must be false
PPOA session signature: EIP-712 typed-data sign referencing active PPOA hash
Session held in memory; all write operations re-verify signature before contract call

5.3 Security Constraints
All RPC calls route through the Association’s private Besu node; no public RPC endpoints
Session tokens are memory-only; page refresh requires full re-authentication
Write operations display a pre-flight summary requiring explicit member confirmation
Rate limiting: 30 write transactions per TAN per rolling 24-hour window
Anomaly detection: three consecutive failed PPOA verifications trigger session lockout and admin alert
Audit log: every action (read + write) logged with wallet, timestamp, module; append-only, IPFS-pinned nightly

8. Conclusion


The House of Grønli ©™ PMA Utility provides a complete, self-contained private administrative infrastructure for sovereign asset management, member identity, and internal settlement. By grounding the system in a pre-existing registered trademark as the immutable identity anchor, and layering ISO 20022-structured messaging over an on-chain double-entry ledger, the architecture achieves:
Cryptographic identity assurance rooted in temporal trademark priority
Non-repudiable administrative authorisation via on-chain PPOA session proofs
Standards-aligned internal messaging that can interface with public networks through a controlled gateway layer
Ring-fenced SPV accounting for the Indre Stambane 5it infrastructure project
Full auditability through IPFS-anchored immutable records at every layer

The system is designed to operate entirely within the private domain of the Association. No member private keys are held by the Association, no public network credentials are used internally, and no regulatory obligations applicable to licensed financial institutions apply to its internal operations.
